# Editorial Design Notes: Flaneur718 About Me Page

## Core Narrative Concept

### The 2015 Hinge Point

**Why this moment is powerful:**
- Specificity and physicality - concrete decision to buy a Nikon D3300
- Before: someone who looked at the world with eyes
- After: someone who looked at the world through a lens
- Peru trip connects photography to movement and exploration from the beginning
- Establishes photography as intertwined with wandering and flâneur sensibility

**Structural approach:**
- Everything before 2015 should feel like leading somewhere without quite arriving
- Everything after 2015 should feel like unfolding and deepening of something already begun

---

## Opening Section

**Visual concept:**
- Burning dollar bill image as opening - provocative and mysterious
- Logo/name overlaid in rough graffiti script style
- Image fades or slides away on scroll, revealing story beginning
- Functions like a book cover or film opening shot
- Does not need to connect literally to Brooklyn or childhood
- Establishes tone and makes a promise about the story

## Chapter 1: Bay Ridge to Academia

### Narrative Scope
- Birth in Bay Ridge, Brooklyn through university years and research work
- Opening line: "They tell me I was born on a snowy February day in Bay Ridge, Brooklyn"
- Covers degrees in English, History, Philosophy, Public Policy
- Work at think tanks and universities, self-teaching code and statistics

### Color Palette
- Blacks, grays, weathered brick tones
- Oxidized green (old copper or chain-link fences)
- Urban and gritty, slightly nostalgic but not precious
- Rooted in New York City

### Scrapbook Elements
- **Found rather than designed** - artifacts from shoeboxes
- Spray paint cap (near graffiti mention)
- Vintage NYC subway map fragment
- Library card
- Composition notebook page with scribbled notes
- Authentically Brooklyn and academic

### Photography
- Brooklyn stoop image with flowers and house number 6602
- Black and white shot in graffitied doorway
- Images literally show environment being described

### Typography & Layout
- Relatively traditional with personality
- Blocks of text with generous margins
- Occasional phrases break free and position differently
- "I roamed the streets and foraged for fun. Some say I still do"
  - Second sentence smaller and offset
  - Like a whispered aside or footnote
  - Breaking fourth wall, connecting past to present
- Movement should be subtle (following structures, educational systems, career paths laid out by others)

## Chapter 2: The Builder (Nook Nook Mats)

### Narrative Scope
- Sustainable home goods brand
- Represents shift in agency - founding, making decisions, learning by doing
- Ends with: "That project ended in 2023"
- Creates implicit question: What came next? What became the thing that didn't end?

### Color Palette
- Warm up slightly from Chapter 1
- Natural earth tones (coconut coir, environmental impact, handcraft)
- Warm browns, natural fiber textures
- Pops of color from Indian textiles or Kochi markets

### Scrapbook Elements
- Objects related to making and commerce
- Shipping label
- Pencil sketch of doormat design
- Jute twine
- Fragment of coconut coir material as texture overlay

### Typography & Layout
- Text moves with more freedom (action, learning, building)
- "I learned logistics, accounting, marketing, and management and just kept going"
  - Kinetic quality
  - Words shifting position slightly on scroll
  - Suggesting momentum and forward motion
- "The experience changed how I saw the world"
  - Different color or weight than surrounding text
  - Moment of realization
  - Foreshadows what comes next
- Section should feel finite and somewhat contained

## Chapter 3: The Peru Transformation (2015)

### Opening Moment - The Hinge Point
- "In 2015, I bought a Nikon D3300 before a trip to Peru"
- **This single sentence deserves dramatic treatment**
- Large, bold type - possibly full screen width
- Breaking across two or three lines
- As you scroll past: words could separate or scatter slightly
- Word "Peru" could leave a color trail - first real vibrant color
- Background shifts from urban grays to warmer, brighter, more saturated
- **Make the threshold crossing visceral and obvious**

### Narrative Elements
- Learning photography through thousands of terrible photos
- Pestering better photographers, watching edits
- Auto to manual, clueless to certain
- Aesthetic emerged through walking, paying attention, shooting
- Learning to see the frames of life

### Color Palette
- Opens up significantly from previous chapters
- No longer Brooklyn concrete and academic gray
- Peru colors, travel vibrancy
- More saturated and warm

### Typography & Layout
- **Genuinely kinetic and playful** (discovery and experimentation)
- "from auto to manual, from clueless to certain"
  - Literally perform transformation on screen
  - "auto" in soft, unfocused letterforms → sharpen to crisp, bold "manual"
  - "clueless" scattered and uncertain → rearranges to "certain" in perfect alignment
- **Not gimmicks but visual metaphors reinforcing narrative**

### Photography
- More dynamic in size and placement
- Tilt at bolder angles
- Overlap slightly like photos spread across table

### Scrapbook Elements
- More photographic in nature
- Camera strap
- Lens cap
- Crumpled bodega bag (protection detail - shows scrappy, make-it-work approach)

## Chapter 4: Mexico City & Film Photography

### Narrative Scope
- "Later, living in Mexico City, I fell in love with 35mm film photography"
- "That city's light, shadow, and color were made for analog capture"
- Philosophy: "Film demands intention; every frame costs something"

### Visual Sophistication
- Even more visually sophisticated than Peru chapter
- Saturated and rich
- Color becomes a major character in the story

### Color Palette
- Warm golden tones
- Dramatic shadows
- If gradually adding color since Peru, Mexico City is where **color dominates**

### Typography & Layout
- Text references light and shadow literally in display
- Certain words brighter while others recede
- Creating depth and dimension on the page itself
- "Film demands intention; every frame costs something"
  - Significant weight and space around it
  - **Thesis statement**
  - Text could leave color trail (ink bleeding or light exposing film)

### Photography
- Film photography work
- Photos of shooting with film cameras
- Given room to breathe and be appreciated

### Scrapbook Elements
- Film canister
- Contact sheet
- Edge of negative strip
- **Rendered more elegantly than Brooklyn graffiti can**
- No longer scrapping and foraging
- Found medium, treating it with respect and intention

## Chapter 5: Flâneur 718 (Current Practice)

### Narrative Tone
- **This section should feel like an arrival**
- Confidence and clarity
- "You know who you are and what you do"

### Color Palette
- Most sophisticated
- Urban grit with intentional pops of color
- Placed exactly where they create maximum impact
- **White space becomes truly generous**

### Typography & Layout
- Bold and declarative
- "Now I document the human condition through street photography throughout this earth"
  - No tricks or animation needed
  - Simply exists on page with authority
- Text can be cleaner and more direct
- **Typography serves as truth without ornamentation**
- No need to convince anyone - simply stating what is true

### Photography
- Strongest street photography work
- Large enough to be appreciated as art
- Various interests: street, architecture, abstractions, landscape
- Writing practice: photo essays, city essays, philosophy, criticism

### Scrapbook Elements
- More implied than literal
- One or two carefully chosen objects
- Camera or notebook
- Positioned with intention rather than scattered casually
- **No longer collecting artifacts from past**
- **Actively creating the work of the present**

### Content Flow
- All flows from central identity as flâneur
- Someone who walks, pays attention, shoots, writes
- Emphasis on **stillness and arrival** rather than continued striving

## Key Design Principles

### Found Rather Than Designed
- Scrapbook elements should feel pulled from shoeboxes
- Authentic artifacts, not polished design elements
- Real details matter more than decorative flourishes

### Kinetic Typography
- Typography that "performs the narrative"
- Effects like typewriter animations, color transformations
- Letterforms embody emotional content
- Movement should enhance storytelling without becoming distracting

### Narrative Arc Through Color
- Progression through distinct color temperatures and palettes
- Mirrors the narrative arc from gray academia to vibrant photography
- Color should support the emotional journey

### Progressive Animation Complexity
- Chapter 1: Subtle movements (following structures)
- Chapter 2: More freedom (building, agency)
- Chapter 3: Kinetic and playful (discovery)
- Chapter 4: Sophisticated (mastery)
- Chapter 5: Restrained confidence (arrival)

### White Space & Breathing Room
- Essential for sophisticated, literary aesthetic
- Becomes more generous as confidence increases
- Chapter 5 has most generous white space

### Authentic Details
- Real film edge codes, actual scrapbook artifacts
- Genuine photography workflow elements
- Create more impact than decorative flourishes
- Visceral authenticity over polish

## Structural Summary

**Five-Chapter Biographical Structure:**
1. **Bay Ridge to Academia** - Urban gray origins
2. **The Builder** - Earth tones, entrepreneurial era
3. **2015 Peru Transformation** - THE HINGE POINT - vibrant awakening
4. **Mexico City Film Mastery** - Saturated sophistication
5. **Flâneur 718** - Confident arrival

**Design Philosophy:**
- Before photography (Chapters 1-2): Leading somewhere without arriving
- The moment (Chapter 3): Visceral transformation
- After photography (Chapters 4-5): Unfolding and deepening mastery

**Emotional Journey:**
Following structures → Building agency → Discovering medium → Mastering craft → Arriving at identity
